Facial Landmark Detection for Non-Verbal Individuals

Utilized C++ and OpenCV to design a facial landmark detection system for patients suffering from complete paralysis to help them communicate. I have designed a virtual keyboard and merged it with the detection code to implement a clicking capability using winks and eyebrow movement. In order to enable fast communication I have used SQL to create a database, storing the relative frequency of the words used. The system was further improved using cascaded neural networks for accuracy.
Microwave Amplifier Design

I have designed, simulated, built, and tested a microwave amplifier at 3.1 GHz frequency in a 50Ω system. To design the circuit, NI-AWR Microwave Office was utilized. The input and output matching networks were designed using open circuited shunt stubs since they can be easily fabricated and allow some tuning. I have calculated the maximum gain of the BJT from the given S parameters in the data sheet, ensured that the amplifier is stable over the entire useful bandwidth of the device, minimized S11 and S22, and maximized the bandwidth. The circuit was then fabricated with RT/Duroid 5880 with a dielectric thickness of 787.41µm. In the final step, I assembled and mounted the circuit on the microwave test jig and tested it with a VNA.
4 Element Microstrip Patch Linear Antenna Array

Utilized NI-AWR Microwave Office to design a microstrip patch antenna element and implemented a 4 element linear array with 5.2GHz frequency. The substrate used in the design is RT/Duroid 5880 with a dielectric thickness of 1.574mm. I have calculated, simulated, and measured the electric field in various planes of polarization and the S parameters. I maximized the input return loss at port 1 of the feed network to ensure that the power from the generator will enter the feed network and be available to the antenna patches. In addition to that I made sure that the array design was matched to 50 Ω with zero reactance, zero reflection, and full transmission.

CMOS Circuit Design

I utilized cadence to design basic inverters and NAND gates of unit size in a 60nm processes technology. This involved designing the overall schematic and then finally layout on the chip. The chip layout was verified to comply with various process technology requirements through the passing of DRC and LVS tests.

Wind Farm and Distribution System Design

I designed a simulated utility-scale 500MW wind power plant that delivers power to the city of Ottawa via a transmission line system using PowerWorld Simulator. I have sized the wind turbines, the cables, and the transformers for stepping up and down voltage. In order to build the transmission line I have utilized the ABCD network representation. Finally, to protect the system against faults and lightning effects, I have designed switchgear including capacitor banks, surge arrestors, circuit breakers, isolators, lightning arresters, earth switches, protection relays, and SCADA systems.

Smart Solar Inverter System and EV Charger Design

Designed a grid-tie solar inverter with 5kW rated output DC power to 120V AC with an electric vehicle charging capability. This charging system was adjustable for different battery bank sizes and types and can charge Lead Acid and Li-ion batteries. In addition to that I designed a smart inverter suitable for solar panels with built-in high-performance maximum power point tracking (MPPT) function to effectively capture and collect sunlight. I had to size the solar panels and determine the way of their connection to produce 5kW.

Home Alarm System

I coded a home alarm system in C that utilized the Freescale/Motorola HCS12. The program involved arming the system using a keypad. When the system is armed by typing in the passcode the alarm can be tripped by tripping a sensor. This will activate an interupt that causes an alarm to sound. The alarm can be disarmed by typing in the same passcode. It also utilized a 7 segment display that showed the passcode when typed.
